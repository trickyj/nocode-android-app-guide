# Build & Launch Your Android App on Play Store
**No-Code Guide for Beginners**
A complete beginner's checklist — from hardware requirements to going live on Google Play. Zero coding experience needed.
### 📊 Quick Stats
 * **Phases:** 6
 * **Min Budget (Tools):** ₹0
 * **Time to Launch:** ~6 weeks
 * **Play Store Fee:** $25 (one-time)
## Phase 0: Hardware & Devices You Need
MUST HAVE
 * [ ] **A Computer or Laptop** Windows, Mac, or Linux. Minimum: 4GB RAM, 50GB free space. Recommended: 8GB RAM.
 * [ ] **An Android Phone** Running Android 8.0 (Oreo) or higher for testing. (Alternatively, use a free online emulator).
 * [ ] **Stable Internet Connection** Minimum 5 Mbps for cloud-based no-code tools.
 * [ ] **A USB Cable** To connect your phone to your computer for testing and file transfer.
## Phase 1: Accounts & Registrations
MOSTLY FREE
 * [ ] **Google Account (Gmail)** Required for Play Console, Firebase, and Drive.
 * [ ] **Google Play Developer Account** Register at play.google.com/console. Requires a one-time **$25 fee** (~₹2,100).
 * [ ] **No-Code App Builder Account** Sign up for MIT App Inventor, Thunkable, or FlutterFlow.
 * [ ] **Canva Account** Used for creating app icons, feature graphics, and screenshots.
 * [ ] **Firebase Account (Optional)** Necessary if your app needs a database or user login.
> 💡 **Tip:** The $25 fee is a one-time payment. Multiple friends can pool money and publish different apps under one developer account to save costs!
> 
## Phase 2: Choose Your No-Code App Builder
| Tool | Best For | Price |
|---|---|---|
| **MIT App Inventor** | Total beginners; schools. | 100% Free |
| **Thunkable** | Beautiful user interfaces. | Free Tier / Paid |
| **Kodular** | Advanced features & AdMob. | Free |
| **FlutterFlow** | Professional/Startup quality. | Free Tier / Paid |
**Recommendation:** Start with **MIT App Inventor** or **Thunkable**. Move to FlutterFlow once you are comfortable.
## Phase 3: Plan Your App
DO THIS FIRST
 * [ ] **Define your idea in one sentence.** (e.g., "A quiz app for my class").
 * [ ] **Wireframe your screens.** Draw Home, Menu, and About screens on paper.
 * [ ] **List 3–5 core features.** Don't overcomplicate Version 1.
 * [ ] **Choose a category.** (Education, Tools, Social, etc.).
 * [ ] **Identify your audience.** Who will use this?
## Phase 4: Build & Test
CORE PHASE
 * [ ] **Watch a tutorial.** Spend 2 hours watching the "Getting Started" video for your tool.
 * [ ] **Build the Home Screen.** Drag and drop labels, buttons, and images.
 * [ ] **Add Logic.** Use "Blocks" to define actions (e.g., "When Button Clicked -> Open Screen 2").
 * [ ] **Enable Developer Mode.** On your phone: Settings > About Phone > Tap "Build Number" 7 times > Enable USB Debugging.
 * [ ] **Live Testing.** Use the companion app for your builder to see changes on your phone instantly.
 * [ ] **Beta Test.** Have 3–5 friends try the app and find bugs.
 * [ ] **Export as .AAB.** Download the Android App Bundle file for Play Store submission.
## Phase 5: Play Store Assets
BEFORE SUBMITTING
 * [ ] **App Icon:** 512 × 512 px PNG.
 * [ ] **Feature Graphic:** 1024 × 500 px.
 * [ ] **Screenshots:** Minimum 2 (Portrait).
 * [ ] **Short Description:** Max 80 characters.
 * [ ] **Full Description:** Up to 4,000 characters explaining features and usage.
 * [ ] **Privacy Policy:** Use a generator and host the link on Google Sites or GitHub.
## Phase 6: Upload & Publish
FINAL STEPS
 1. **Sign In:** Go to the Play Console.
 2. **Create App:** Fill in the basic app name and language info.
 3. **Store Listing:** Upload all assets from Phase 5.
 4. **App Release:** Upload your .aab file under the "Production" track.
 5. **Content Rating:** Complete the questionnaire to get an age rating.
 6. **Price & Distribution:** Set the app to "Free" and select countries (e.g., India).
 7. **Submit:** Click "Send for Review."
> ⏳ **Note:** Review for new accounts usually takes **3–7 days**.
> 
## 🗓️ 6-Week Roadmap
 * **Week 1:** Setup & Learning (Tutorials + "Hello World").
 * **Week 2:** Plan & Design (Wireframes + Canva assets).
 * **Week 3:** Build Core Features (Logic + Screens).
 * **Week 4:** Polish & Beta Test (Bug fixing).
 * **Week 5:** Prepare Listing (Privacy policy + Descriptions).
 * **Week 6:** Submit & Go Live! 🚀
